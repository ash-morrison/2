How Chains Are Rethinking Backlist Placement

A closer look at planograms, turns, and the economics of discovery.

By Staff Reporter January 30, 2026 New York, NY

Planograms vs. Serendipity

Chain retailers increasingly rely on tighter planograms to balance discovery and efficiency. Endcaps and tables prioritize frontlist velocity, while deep backlist migrates online.

Merchandisers say the goal is not to shrink selection, but to position inventory where it turns and free space for launches.

Data-Driven Curation

POS data informs which titles earn physical slots—and how long they keep them. The threshold for keeping space rises as freight and labor costs climb.

For publishers, the message is clear: align marketing and co-op with retail windows that matter, or risk missing the store cycle entirely.
